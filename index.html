Hooke's law



The equation in Hooke's Law can be positive when the spring is stretched, meaning the displacement "x" is positive, and the restoring force acts in the opposite direction to return it to equilibrium.


represents the length of the spring when it is stretched. This is the measurement of the spring after it has been extended from its natural resting position, indicating the force applied to elongate it.
